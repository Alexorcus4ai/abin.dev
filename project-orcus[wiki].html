Orcus.wiki: A Hub for Aspiring Entrepreneurs

Orcus.wiki served as a hub for aspiring entrepreneurs, offering:

Event Search

Filter startup workshops, meetups, and conferences by area and date.

News Digests

Daily or weekly curated updates on funding, launches, and ecosystem trends.

Glossary

Searchable definitions of key startup terms, updated regularly.

Reach

About 4,000 monthly visitors seeking practical resources.

Why It Ended

Resource Constraints

Managing hosting costs, APIs, and ongoing maintenance alone became overwhelming alongside other commitments.

Content & Moderation Load

Manual curation of news and vetting event submissions demanded significant time. Automating risked quality or legal issues.

Solo Management

Without collaborators or partnerships, feature requests (forums, APIs, personalized tools) could not be implemented sustainably.

No Clear Monetization

Balancing free access with funding needs (ads, sponsorships, premium features) proved challenging for a solo side project.

Key Takeaways

Start Lean

Focus on a minimal viable feature (e.g., just event search or a newsletter) before expanding.

Automate Wisely

Use official APIs or RSS for data ingestion but keep quality checks.

Build a Team/Community

Even a small group or volunteers can share curation, moderation, and development tasks.

Plan Sustainability

Explore sponsorships, partnerships, or light monetization early to cover costs.

Graceful Shutdown

If pausing, notify users, archive essential content (e.g., glossary), and consider open-sourcing to let others continue.

Moving Forward

If you wish to relaunch or build a successor:

  • Validate user needs first (surveys, interviews).
  • Leverage no-code tools or managed services to reduce maintenance.
  • Seek collaborators or partnerships (incubators, universities).
  • Consider hyper-local focus (e.g., Kerala startup events) or niche verticals.
  • Plan for gradual growth: start small, gather feedback, iterate.
